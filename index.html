FAQ

Questions parents ask first.

Is APEX state-approved?

We're in the TDLR approval track for Texas (the largest single drivers-ed market in the US). Adjacent states follow. We will never market a course as state-approved before it actually is.

How does "no timers" work if my state requires X hours?

Texas law (TDLR Rule 84) requires minimum total course hours, not per-page timers — that's a competitor convention, not a state mandate. We track cumulative active engagement: scenarios, decisions, quizzes, live-driving footage. Idle time pauses the clock. Total time-on-task always meets state minimums (24 hours teen · 32 hours parent-taught · 6 hours adult). One TDLR "instructional hour" = 55 minutes.

Will my teen actually use this?

Streaks, XP, friend leaderboards, and bite-sized lessons mean APEX gets opened the way Duolingo gets opened. The Duolingo retention model lifted user retention from 12% → 55%. We're applying the same framework.

Does this replace behind-the-wheel hours?

No course can. APEX covers the full theoretical/classroom requirement and gives parents a behind-the-wheel companion: drive logging, daily scenario prompts to use in the car, and a progress dashboard. Real road time still happens with you.

What's the catch on the price?

There isn't one. One charge. No subscriptions. No auto-enrolled add-ons. No surprise roadside-assistance subscriptions like a competitor's BBB record will show. If we ever change pricing, current students pay nothing more.
