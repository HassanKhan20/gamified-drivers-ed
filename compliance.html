What APEX won't do.

A list of practices we've audited from competitors' BBB filings and refused to copy.

✗

Auto-enroll your minor in a recurring subscription

A Texas competitor ties their discounted course price to a 30-day "free" Allstate Roadside trial that renews at $5/mo, with cancellation only available via the minor's login. We don't do this.

Reference: BBB complaints citing FTC ROSCA & TX Bus & Comm §17.46(b)(24)
✗

Use "minister's discounts" — a fake list price next to a fake sale price

Competitors list "$115 → $74 today only" permanently. We don't. Our price is the price.

✗

Expire your purchased course after a year

Industry-standard contracts let providers void a course you paid for at day 366 with no notice. Lifetime access is non-negotiable for us.

✗

Force per-page timers that lock you on a slide for 60 seconds

TDLR rules require minimum total hours, not per-slide timers — that's a competitor invention. We track real engagement; idle time pauses the clock; quiz wins count toward your hours.

Reference: TDLR Rule 84, Texas Education Code Ch. 1001 Subchapter C
✗

Charge processing fees on refunds

Some industry players keep 10% as a "processing fee" on refunds. Ours are 100% within the 14-day window.

✗

Sell or share your data with marketing partners

Student progress data stays inside APEX. The only third-party we share with is the state agency that issues your certificate.
